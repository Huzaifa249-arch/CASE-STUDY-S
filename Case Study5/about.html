About Indian Premier League

The biggest cricketing extravaganza in the world

History

The Indian Premier League (IPL) is a professional Twenty20 cricket league in India contested during March or April and May of every year by eight teams representing eight different cities in India. The league was founded by the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) in 2007 and has since become the most-attended cricket league in the world and ranked sixth among all sports leagues.

First Season: 2008

Champions: Rajasthan Royals

Most Successful Team: Mumbai Indians (5 titles)

Tournament Format

The tournament format involves each team playing every other team twice in a round-robin format - once at home and once away. After the league stage:

  • Top 4 teams qualify for the playoffs
  • Qualifier 1: 1st vs 2nd (winner goes to final)
  • Eliminator: 3rd vs 4th (loser eliminated)
  • Qualifier 2: Loser of Qualifier 1 vs Winner of Eliminator
  • Final: Winner of Qualifier 1 vs Winner of Qualifier 2

Rules & Regulations

  • Team Composition: Each team can have a maximum of 25 players, with at least 16 Indian players
  • Playing XI: Maximum 4 overseas players in the playing eleven
  • Super Over: In case of a tie in playoffs or final, a Super Over determines the winner
  • Strategic Time-out: Two 2.5-minute strategic time-outs per innings (between overs 6-9 and 13-16)
  • DRS: Decision Review System available with one unsuccessful review per innings
  • Impact Player: Teams can substitute one player during the match
  • Salary Cap: Each team has a salary cap of INR 95 crore for player contracts

Awards & Recognition

  • Orange Cap: Awarded to the highest run-scorer of the season
  • Purple Cap: Awarded to the highest wicket-taker of the season
  • Most Valuable Player: Best performer of the season based on overall contribution
  • Fairplay Award: Team with the best conduct on the field
  • Emerging Player: Best young player (under 25) of the season
